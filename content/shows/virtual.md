## The Virtual Experience

The pandemic taught Victor something unexpected: the camera removes distance but preserves astonishment. Watching someone reach through a screen and apparently read your thoughts is, if anything, stranger than watching it happen in person — because there is nowhere for the explanation to hide.

Victor's virtual shows were developed during 2020 and have since been delivered to audiences in over thirty countries via Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, and bespoke event platforms. They require no special software on the audience's end and work with any consumer-grade webcam setup.

### How It Works

Participants connect to a video call as they would any virtual meeting. Victor runs the session as host, directing attention and engagement as he would from a stage. Effects are designed specifically for the medium — leveraging, rather than fighting against, the realities of video: delayed reactions, split-screen attention, and the strange intimacy of seeing someone's face fill your monitor.

Audience members are invited to participate via chat, raised hands, or direct video interaction. Victor routinely reads information that participants have only thought about and never typed. The experience is consistently described as stranger than people anticipate.

### Formats

**Virtual Close-Up (10–30 guests)** — A highly intimate session with maximum participant interaction. Ideal for team events, leadership offsites, and client entertainment.

**Virtual Keynote (up to 500 guests)** — A broadcast-style performance combining pre-submitted audience data with live interaction. Suitable for large all-hands meetings, virtual conferences, and online product launches.

### Technical Requirements

Victor handles all hosting and technical setup. Clients receive a simple briefing document for their IT teams. No additional software, plugins, or audience preparation is required.
